# StudySpot User Testing Guide

The course requires at least five real users who are not members of the project
team. Use anonymized tester IDs only and do not collect unnecessary personal
information.

## Suggested Scenario

Ask each tester to imagine they need a place for an upcoming study session near
BINUS Alam Sutera. They should:

1. Enter their genuine study preferences.
2. Request five recommendations.
3. Review the ranking, place details, and explanations.
4. Open at least one Google Maps link.
5. Complete the feedback questions.

## Quantitative Questions

Use a 1-5 scale:

- The preference questions were easy to understand.
- The recommendations felt relevant to my needs.
- The explanations helped me understand the recommendations.
- The interface was easy to use.
- I would use StudySpot when choosing a study place.

## Qualitative Questions

- Which recommendation was most useful, and why?
- Was any result surprising or unsuitable?
- Which information or feature was missing?
- What is the single most important improvement?

## Analysis for the PPT

Report the anonymized tester background, usage scenario, mean score for each
Likert question, notable positive and negative themes, changes made after
testing, and limitations. Avoid claiming broad usability from only five users.
